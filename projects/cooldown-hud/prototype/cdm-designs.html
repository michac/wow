Cooldown HUD · design study

Demonology CDM — five directions

Five takes on a condensed, no-icon cooldown board for a Demonology Warlock, all driven by one simulated rotation. Vertical space = priority, horizontal space = grouping (Tyrant lines up with the demons it empowers). Every animation here sticks to what the live 12.0 addon can actually do — nothing reads a value the game keeps secret.

Live we read & drive it in Lua — shards, cap glitter, proc presence · Borrowed Blizzard's secure widget draws it — cooldown swipe, DoT timers
timescale ×3 · illustrative

1 · Priority Column

vertical rank · grouped burst

Top = press-soonest. The shard rail flips generate→spend; the burst trio (own-timer tracked) ramps up as Tyrant nears ready and the lane lights as one during the window. A big CORE [X]/4 sits above utility.

2 · Burst Lane

horizontal alignment first

The window is the hero: Tyrant + Dreadstalkers + Fel Ravager align on one rail; the lane pulses together while the Tyrant window is up (detected by a buff's presence) — "dump now." Their individual swipes are Blizzard's.

3 · Resource-Centric

shards as the anchor

Soul Shards are the one thing we truly own, so they're the focal point — big, central, glittering at cap. Abilities orbit as small pips.

4 · Compact Dashboard

dense · glanceable grid

Cockpit density: a shard strip, a fixed cooldown grid, and borrowed DoT bars underneath. Fixed slots build muscle memory.

5 · Clear the Board

salience = actionability

Only the actionable thing is bright; anything on cooldown or irrelevant fades almost to nothing. An empty board means you're playing optimally — the goal is to keep clearing it.

Solid-color styling, no icons — identity is color group + position + a 4-letter label (legible desaturated). Untracked cooldowns are a constant color with only Blizzard's borrowed swipe. The burst trio (Tyrant/Dreadstalkers/Fel Ravager) also ramps up as it nears ready — we detect the cast (spellID is readable) and run our own base-cooldown timer. Also live & ours: the shard rail's generate→spend recolor + cap glitter, and Demonic CORE [X]/4 (X is Blizzard's stack number we enlarge; “/4” is static — we can't read X).
